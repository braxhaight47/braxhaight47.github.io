General Overview:

Magic the Gathering is a trading card game that is played by millions around the world.

The game focuses on two to four players, depending in the game mode, and players will fight until the life points of their oppenents hit zero.

This is done by summoning creatures, playing sorceries, casting instants, and a multitude of other cards that fit your stradegy.

In Magic the Gathering, there are five colors, these colors dennote different factions and by combining the different colors you create a unique stradegy.

Each page on this site will go over the general concept of each color and some personalities that match them as well to help you find your color!


Brief Overview of the Colors:

  • White: The color of peace, utilizing balence and lifegain to gain an advantage; features knights, angels, and other holy creatures.

  • Blue: The color of control, manipulating the battlefield through counter spells and extra draw; features sea creatues, wizards, and tricksters.

  • Black: The color of death, destroys others without mercy while resurecting their own; features zombies, necromancers, and the undead.

  • Red: The color of burn, speedily deals consistant bits of damage whitiling down their enemies; features goblins, fireballs, and dragons.

  • Green: The color of growth, using small creatures to create giants that trample over their foes; features giants, elves, and titans.


White

Playstyle:

White is the color of peace and justice, as such there are ample cards that fit both titles.

With the aspect of peace, white specializes in evening out the playing feild, as well as granting lifegain to its user.

With the aspect of justice, white excels at exiling oppenents cards- meaning banishing them from the feild so they can no longer be used.

By combining these traits white shines as a solid defender, keeping themseleves alive while striking their enemies with holy justice if needed.

If being balanced between attack and defense, sustainability, and justice are important to you then white is probably the color for you!



Card Types:

White has a large amount of holy creatures, the majority being focused on defense.

Knights primarily focus on creating a defense and work on buffing each other to overwhelm the enemy.

Angels focus on healing and flying to out last and go around their enemies defenses.

Spirits focus on amassing their forces and stealing from those who wrong them to swarm foes.

These creatures are the core of white and if they sound like your thing then white is for you!

Blue

Playstyle:

Blue is the color of control and manipulation, as such there are ample cards that fit both titles.

With the aspect of control, blue specializes in countering spells before they hit the battlefield, letting their oppenents only do what they want.

With the aspect of manipulation, blue excels at changing, tapping, or morphing their oppenents cards to gain advantage.

By combining these traits blue shines as a witty manipulator, keeping their oppenents unsure of their own actions.

If being in control and messing with your oppenents are important to you then blue is probably the color for you!



Card Types:

Blue has a large amount of tricksters and wizards, the majority being focused on advantage.

Wizards primarily focus on either buffing spells or generating more cards when spells are cast.

Sea creatures focus on landing large hits or on generating card draw when they hit the enemy.

Griffins focus on creating large amounts of card draw and flying over enemys.

These creatures, combined with spells, are the core of blue and if they sound like your thing then blue is for you!

Black

Playstyle:

Black is the color of death and destruction, as such there are ample cards that fit both titles.

With the aspect of death, black specializes in bringing cards back to life and using health as a resource.

With the aspect of destruction, black excels at destorying oppenents cards- meaning sending them to the graveyard (maybe to let your bring them to your side).

By combining these traits black shines as a unique attacker, constantly attacking and bringing back their forces while whitling down foes.

If manipulating the graveyard and destorying your enemies outright are important to you then black is probably the color for you!



Card Types:

Black has a large amount of zombies and gouls, the majority being focused on revival abilities.

Zombies primarily focus on either coming back to life or being buffed from cards reviving.

Gouls focus on stealing cards from the foe or making them discard cards.

The key aspect is creating situations where lots of creatures die across the board but only yours come back.

These creatures, combined with board wipes, are the core of black and if they sound like your thing then black is for you!

Red

Playstyle:

Red is the color of speed and damage, as such there are ample cards that fit both titles.

With the aspect of speed, red specializes in getting out a lot of small cards that can strike instantly.

With the aspect of damage, red excels at damage spells that burn the enemy to deal true damage.

By comnining these traits red shines as a fast offense, keeping their oppenents on the defense with a stream of attacks.

If being a brute force attacker is important to you then red is probably the color for you!



Card Types:

Red has a large amount of goblins and elementals, the majority being focused on damage.

Goblins primarily focus on attacking instantly and for minor damage.

Elementals focus on hitting hard and instantly, but die afterwards.

Burn spells deal true damage to foes and can help push your enemy back from their own offense.

These creatures, combined with burn spells, are the core of red and if they sound like your thing then red is for you!

Green

Playstyle:

Green is the color of growth and force, as such there are ample cards that fit both titles.

With the aspect of growth, green specializes in buffing their own creatures and gaining lots of mana (resource).

With the aspect of force, green excels at playing large monsters that trample over oppenents defenses.

By comnining these traits green shines as a steadily growing attacker, slowly ramping up to large unstoppable forces.

If being a large looming threat is important to you then green is probably the color for you!



Card Types:

Green has a large amount of titans and animals, the majority being focused on gaining mana advantage then playing large creatures.

Titans primarily focus on trampling over all forces your oppenets try and put in your way.

Animals tend to be used to gain mana advantage in order to prepare for the large creatures.

Elves focus on creating large amounts of card draw and mana advantage.

These creatures are the core of green and if they sound like your thing then green is for you!
